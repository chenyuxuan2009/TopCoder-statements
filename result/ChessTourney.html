Problem Statement for "ChessTourney"

Problem Statement

John is a chess player and is going to compete in the upcoming tournament. When a player loses a game, he immediately leaves the tournament. Only the player who can win all his games will become the winner.

During the tournament John may win some prizes given in a int[] winnings. Element 0 of winnings represents the prize John gets for just entering the tournament, and element i of winnings (for i > 0) represents the amount John gets for winning his game in the i-th round. For example, if John wins the first two games and loses the third one, he'll get winnings[0] for entering the tournament, winnings[1] for winning his game in the first round (which grants him a spot in round 2) and winnings[2] for winning his second game and advancing to round 3. Losing in round 3 will give him nothing.

You've analyzed the play-off tree and now you know John's chances of advancing through each of the rounds, given in a int[] odds. Element i of odds represents the probability (as a percentage) that John will win his game in round (i + 1). Return John's expected winnings in the tournament.

Definition

Class:
ChessTourney
Method:
expectedWinning
Parameters:
int[], int[]
Returns:
double
Method signature:
double expectedWinning(int[] winnings, int[] odds)
(be sure your method is public)

Notes

  • The returned value must be accurate to within a relative or absolute value of 1E-9.

Constraints

  • winnings will contain between 2 and 50 elements, inclusive.
  • odds will contain exactly n - 1 elements, where n is the number of elements in winnings.
  • Each element of winnings will be between 0 and 1000, inclusive.
  • Each element of odds will be between 0 and 100, inclusive.
