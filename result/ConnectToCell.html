Problem Statement for "ConnectToCell"

Problem Statement

When a user turns on his cellular phone, the phone must be connected to one of the cell towers. You will be given two int[]s - x and y, giving you the cartesian coordinates for all cell towers. You will be also given a int[] power, representing powers for all cells (ith elements of x, y and power represent a single tower). Assuming that the phone is located at point (0, 0), you must find the 0-based index of the nearest tower. In case of a tie, the most powerful one must be chosen. If the tie still exists, choose the cell tower with the smallest index.

Definition

Class:
ConnectToCell
Method:
findNearest
Parameters:
int[], int[], int[]
Returns:
int
Method signature:
int findNearest(int[] x, int[] y, int[] power)
(be sure your method is public)

Notes

  • In the plane, the distance between points (x1, y1) and (x2, y2) is equal to sqrt((x1 - x2)2 + (y1 - y2)2), where sqrt stands for a square root.

Constraints

  • x, y and power will all have the same number of elements.
  • x, y and power will each contain between 2 and 50 elements, inclusive.
  • All elements of x and y will be between -10000 and 10000, inclusive.
  • All elements of power will be between 1 and 10000, inclusive.
  • No two cells will be located at the same point.
