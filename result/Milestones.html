Problem Statement for "Milestones"

Problem Statement

A straight road goes from city A to city B. The length of the road is d miles. There are n milestones along the road, located at distance p[0], p[1], ..., p[n-1] from city A.

You must keep exactly k milestones and remove the others. You would like to do this in such a way that the maximal distance between two adjacent milestones, or a city and the closest milestone, is as small as possible.

Return the minimal possible maximal distance.

Definition

Class:
Milestones
Method:
maxDistance
Parameters:
int, int[], int
Returns:
int
Method signature:
int maxDistance(int d, int[] p, int k)
(be sure your method is public)

Constraints

  • d will be between 1 and 100,000, inclusive.
  • p will contain between 1 and 50 elements, inclusive.
  • Each element in p will be between 1 and d - 1, inclusive.
  • Elements of p will form a strictly ascending sequence.
  • k will be between 1 and the number of elements in p, inclusive.

The maximal distance in this case is 6. If you keep milestone 1, the distances are 5 and 5, respectively, and the maximal distance is 5. The second option is better, so the answer is 5.
