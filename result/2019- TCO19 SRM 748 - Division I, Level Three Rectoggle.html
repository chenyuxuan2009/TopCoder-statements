Problem Statement for "Rectoggle"

Problem Statement

Rectoggle is a game for two players. The game is played on a huge rectangular LED panel. Both rows and columns of the panel are numbered starting from 0 in the top left corner. The players take alternating turns. Each turn looks as follows:

  1. Select any rectangular region with at most maxrows rows, at most maxcols columns, and a lit LED in the bottom right corner.
  2. Toggle all LEDs within the selected region.

The player who cannot make a valid move (because all LEDs are off) loses the game.

You are given the int[]s ledrow and ledcol: coordinates of the lit LEDs at the beginning of the game. More precisely, for each valid i there is a lit LED in row ledrow[i] column ledcol[i]; and there are no other lit LEDs. You are also given the limits maxrows and maxcols. Return 1 if the first player wins and 2 if the second player wins, assuming both play the game optimally.

Constraints

  • ledrow will have between 0 and 200 elements, inclusive.
  • ledcol will have the same number of elements as ledrow.
  • Each element of ledrow and ledcol will be between 0 and 10^5, inclusive.
  • The coordinates of LEDs described by ledrow and ledcol will be distinct.
  • maxrows and maxcols will each be between 1 and 10^5, inclusive.
