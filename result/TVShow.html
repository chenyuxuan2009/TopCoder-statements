Problem Statement for "TVShow"

Problem Statement

You are on a TV game show where two players try to maximize their earnings. Initially, your opponent has X0 dollars and you have Y0 dollars. Before the game starts, your opponent chooses a number A and you choose a number B (A &gt; 0, B &gt; 0).

The game consists of n rounds. Each round of the game proceeds as follows. Let X be the amount of money your opponent has at the beginning of the round, and let Y be the amount of money you have. Your opponent will choose between two options. He can either turn his money into X+A dollars and turn your money into Y*B dollars, or he can turn his money into X*B dollars and turn your money into Y+A dollars. All operations are done with real numbers. The amount of money each player has at the end of n rounds is that player's final earnings.

Your opponent will play in such a way that maximizes his final earnings. If, during a round, both of his options would lead to the same final earnings for himself, he will choose the first option (turn his money into X+A dollars and turn your money into Y*B dollars).

Your only choice in the game is the value of B. Return the maximum possible final earnings you can achieve.

Definition

Class:
TVShow
Method:
maximalWin
Parameters:

Returns:
double
Method signature:
double maximalWin(int n, double X0, double Y0, double A)

Notes

  • The returned value must have an absolute or relative error less than 1e-9.

Constraints

  • n will be between 1 and 100, inclusive.
  • X0 will be between 1 and 1000, inclusive.
  • Y0 will be between 1 and 1000, inclusive.
  • A will be between 1 and 1000, inclusive.
