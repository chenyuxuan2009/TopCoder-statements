Problem Statement for "BrokenTable"

Problem Statement

You want to buy a table with its top being a convex polygon. The vertices of this polygon are described in clockwise order in int[]s x and y. The coordinate of vertex i is (x[i], y[i]).

Unfortunately, the company, which produces tables, is known for very bad quality. Therefore each corner of the table-top may equiprobably have or don't have a table-leg. Table is called stable if there are at least 3 table corners with legs and if table-top's centre of gravity is located strictly inside of the convex hull of all table corners with legs. Return the probability of the table being stable.

Definition

Class:
BrokenTable
Method:
probStable
Parameters:
int[], int[]
Returns:
double
Method signature:
double probStable(int[] x, int[] y)
(be sure your method is public)

Notes

  • You may assume that the table-top has a constant density and the events "corner i has a leg" are totally independent.
  • A return value with either an absolute or relative error of less than 1.0E-9 is considered correct.

Constraints

  • x will contain between 3 and 50 elements, inclusive.
  • y will contain the same number of elements as x.
  • Each element of x and y will be between -100 and 100, inclusive.
  • The points (x[i], y[i]) will be the vertices of a convex polygon, given in clockwise order.
  • No three consecutive table corners will lie on the same straight line.
  • The distance between table-top's centre of gravity and any straight line, which passes through some points (x[i], y[i]) and (x[j], y[j]), will be at least 1.0E-6.
