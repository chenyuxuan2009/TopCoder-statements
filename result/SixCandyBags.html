Problem Statement for "SixCandyBags"

Problem Statement

There is a vending machine that sells candy. It contains N distinct bags of candy, numbered 0 to N-1. Bag i contains candies[i] candies and costs price[i] cents.

You want to purchase exactly wantPieces of candy. You have exactly haveMoney cents.

You are aware that the vending machine is faulty: after the sixth purchase it becomes stuck and remains stuck until fixed by a technician. It's Sunday, so once you break the machine, that's it. Thus, you can purchase at most six bags of candy.

More expensive candies are fancier and taste better. Thus, if you can reach your main goal, you want to maximize the total cost of your purchase.

If you cannot purchase the exact right total number of candies, return -1. Otherwise, return the maximum cost of a valid way to purchase that many candies.

Definition

Class:
SixCandyBags
Method:
buy
Parameters:
int[], int[], int, int
Returns:
int
Method signature:
int buy(int[] candies, int[] price, int wantPieces, int haveMoney)
(be sure your method is public)

Constraints

  • candies will have between 1 and 100 elements, inclusive.
  • Each element of candies will be between 1 and 10^9, inclusive.
  • price will have the same number of elements as candies.
  • Each element of price will be between 1 and 10^9, inclusive.
  • wantPieces will be between 1 and 10^9, inclusive.
  • haveMoney will be between 1 and 10^9, inclusive.
