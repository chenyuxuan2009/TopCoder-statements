Problem Statement for "Boats"

Problem Statement

A number of famous problems ask about various creatures trying to get across a river, with only one boat. One version of the problem goes something like this:

There is one boat, with a maximum capacity of maxWeight kilograms. There are a number of different creatures, each of whom has an enemies list and a weight. The creatures may coexist on either shore of the river without fighting, but if any creature is on a boat with any of his or her enemies, there will be trouble. Also, the boat may never carry a group of one or more creatures whose combined weight exceeds the maximum capacity.

Given that navigating the boat across the river requires at least one creature, and that the boat starts on the same side as all of the creatures, you are to return the minimum number of times the boat must go across the river before all the creatures can reach the other side. If it is impossible to get all of the creatures from one side to the other, return -1.

Each creature's enemy list and weight will be represented by corresponding elements of enemies and weights, respectively. Each element of enemies will be a space delimited list of indexes (starting from 0) corresponding to other creatures.

Definition

Class:
Boats
Method:
minCrossings
Parameters:
String[], int[], int
Returns:
int
Method signature:
int minCrossings(String[] enemies, int[] weights, int maxWeight)
(be sure your method is public)

Constraints

  • enemies and weights will contain the same number of elements.
  • enemies and weights will both contain between 1 and 16 elements, inclusive.
  • maxWeight will be between 1 and 1,000,000, inclusive.
  • Each element of weights will be between 1 and 1,000,000, inclusive.
  • Each element of enemies will be a single space delimited list (no leading or trailing spaces) of distinct integers between 0 and the length of enemies - 1 with no extra leading 0's.
  • No element of enemies will contain the same integer as its index (i.e. no one will be his or her own enemy).
