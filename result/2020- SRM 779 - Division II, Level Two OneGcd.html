Problem Statement for "OneGcd"

Problem Statement

The greatest common divisor of two positive integers p and q, denoted gcd(p,q), is the largest d such that d divides p and d divides q. Two positive integers p and q are called coprime if their greatest common divisor is 1. For example, 15 and 44 are coprime but 21 and 35 are not (because gcd(21,35) = 7).

You are given N queries. The parameters for these queries are given in the long[] X and in the int[] Y. These contain N elements each.

For each valid i, answer the following query: Let x = X[i] and y = Y[i]. Consider the closed interval [x, x+y-1]. How many numbers in this interval are coprime with y?

Return a int[] with N elements: for each valid i, the answer to the query given by X[i] and Y[i].

Definition

Class:
OneGcd
Method:
solve
Parameters:
long[], int[]
Returns:
int[]
Method signature:
int[] solve(long[] X, int[] Y)
(be sure your method is public)

Notes

  • The values in Y are guaranteed to have a special form, as described by the last constraint. (Note that the problem can also be solved without this constraint.)

Constraints

  • N will be between 1 and 500, inclusive.
  • X will have exactly N elements.
  • Y will have exactly N elements.
  • All numbers in X and Y will be positive.
  • For each valid i, Y[i] will be at most 10^7.
  • For each valid i, X[i] + Y[i] will be at most 10^18.
  • For each valid i, Y[i] will not be divisible by any prime greater than 10. (I.e., each Y[i] will have the form 2^a * 3^b * 5^c * 7^d, for some a, b, c, d >= 0.)
