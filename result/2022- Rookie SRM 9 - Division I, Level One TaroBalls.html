Problem Statement for "TaroBalls"

Problem Statement

Cat Taro has a box with exactly R red balls and B blue balls inside. He and his friend decided to play the following game.

Players take alternate turns, Taro goes first. In each turn, the current player should take exactly one ball from the box. The only constraint is that the color of the ball he took must be different from the color of the previously taken ball (i.e., the one taken by the opponent in the immediately preceding turn). (Note that in the first turn Taro can take a ball of any color.) The player that cannot take a valid turn loses. Both players play optimally.

You are given the two ints R and B. Return "Taro" if Taro wins the game and "Friend" otherwise (quotes for clarity).

Constraints
