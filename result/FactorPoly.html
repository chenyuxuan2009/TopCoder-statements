Problem Statement for "FactorPoly"

Problem Statement

You will be given a String poly representing a polynomial in a single variable, x. poly will be represented as the sum of a number of terms of the form "ax^k", which represents x to the kth power, times a coefficient, a (which may be negative). Furthermore, each term will be separated from the next term by a single '+' and no two terms will have the same power. Thus, "5x^4+3x^1+9x^0" is an example of a valid polynomial.

Your task is to divide out all factors of the polynomial of the form "ax^1+bx^0", for integers a and b (which may be negative or 0), and return the result in the same format that it is given to you. A polynomial, A, is a factor of another polynomial, C, if there exists a polynomial, B, such that A*B=C, where all of the coefficients of A, B, and C are integers. In the returned String, you should not include any terms of the form "0x^k" and the term with the highest power should have a positive coefficient. The terms in the returned String should also be sorted in descending order by power.

For example, if given "1x^3+4x^2+4x^1+3x^0", you should remove the factor "1x^1+3x^0", to get "1x^2+1x^1+1x^0", since "1x^3+4x^2+4x^1+3x^0" is the product of "1x^1+3x^0" and "1x^2+1x^1+1x^0". "1x^2+1x^1+1x^0" has no valid factors, and so it is returned.

(be sure your method is public)

Notes

  • Beware of overflow!

Constraints

  • poly will be a list of terms formatted as "ax^k", separated by single '+' characters, where a and k are integers.
  • a in each term will not be 0, and will be between -99 and 99, inclusive, and will contain no leading '0's.
  • k in each term will be between 0 and 7, inclusive, and will contain no extra leading '0's.
  • poly will contain between 4 and 50 characters, inclusive.
  • No two terms will have the same power (k).
